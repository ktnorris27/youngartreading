Young Art Reading (YAR) is brand new art competition and exhibition for children and young people in Berkshire. The ‘Young Art’ concept first started in London where a volunteer-led competition and exhibition has been running annually since 1987 and last year raised £86,000 for Cancer Research UK. Volunteers have since replicated the competition in East Anglia, raising £120,000 for Cancer Research UK since their first exhibition 2003, Oxford, raising over £100,000 since 2008, and Devon and Cornwall, who held their first exhibition in 2018. Inspired by this success, a local volunteer committee will be launching a brand new Young Art Reading competition in Berkshire in 2019/20.

Young Art Reading is a local community fundraising event, which will bring together schools, families, local organisations and community groups, to showcase the creativity of Reading’s children and young people while raising money for Cancer Research UK (Registered Charity No. 089464). For children and young people the competition is a great way to demonstrate their creative talents, offering an exciting opportunity to have their work judged by experts and exhibited in public while raising money for Cancer Research’s ‘Kids and Teens’ campaign, which aims to accelerate progress, find new cures and kinder treatments for children’s cancers.

Each year the YAR Committee will choose a theme for the competition and work with schools across Berkshire to encourage children and young people (aged between 4 and 14 years) to create and submit a picture inspired by this theme. For 2019/20, the theme for the brand new competition will be ‘My Reading’. To participate, students donate £2 to Cancer Research UK. Volunteers work with schools to launch the competition and collect students’ artwork for judging. An expert panel of judges then review the artwork, choosing entries from each participating school to exhibit and selecting an overall winner for each age group. The exhibition will take place in the University of Reading’s historic Great Hall, across-two days, with a private opening and prize presentation for families, friends, school representatives and event sponsors and opportunities for members of the public, schools and local community groups to view the exhibition. The exhibition raises money for Cancer Research UK in a variety of ways, including donations for: exhibition programmes; tote bags, postcards and canvasses of children’s artwork; and the mounted and/or framed picture (reserved for families of competition entrants only).
